### Phase 2: Linear Regression — IN PROGRESS 🔄

**Theory understood:**
- Model: `y_pred = X @ w + b`
- Loss (MSE): `mean((y_pred - y)²)`
- Gradient for w: `(2/n) * X.T @ (y_pred - y)`
- Gradient for b: `(2/n) * sum(y_pred - y)`
- Update rule: `w = w - lr * dw`
- Normalization: `X_norm = (X - mean) / std`

**Files implemented:**
- `src/mlzero/supervised/regression/linear.py` — full class, gradient descent, 24 tests pass

## Compound Learnings So Far

These are insights discovered during learning. New Claude sessions should build on these:

- **Learning rate:** too high → loss oscillates/explodes. too low → converges very slowly. Start at 0.01 on normalized data.
- **Normalization:** Always normalize multi-feature data. Use TRAIN stats on both train and test.
- **MSE gradient:** `(2/n) * X.T @ error` is just the chain rule in matrix form.
- **Overfitting check:** Always compare train R² vs test R². Large gap = model memorized training data.
- **sklearn match:** Our gradient descent matches sklearn's closed-form solver within ~3% when epochs are sufficient.
- **Test split:** Never evaluate on training data. Train R² ≠ ability to generalize.
